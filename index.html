Ranking Formula

Each school receives a composite score from 1–5 based on four weighted factors:

Factor Weight Why This Weight
Vet School Quality & Access 35% The entire purpose of undergrad is to get into vet school
4-Year Cost 35% Must still afford 4 years of vet school after undergrad
Admission Likelihood 10% Most schools are accessible; low weight avoids over-penalizing reaches
Plan B Strength 20% If vet school doesn't happen, the degree should still have strong career value

Formula: Score = (Vet × 0.35) + (Cost × 0.35) + (Admission × 0.10) + (Plan B × 0.20)

Admission Categories

Each school is classified as SAFETY, MATCH, or REACH based on how Avery's profile compares:

Category Criteria
SAFETY Acceptance rate >70%, or Avery's 3.85 GPA and ACT 27 are well above the school's middle 50% range
MATCH Acceptance rate 40–70%, GPA and ACT near or within the school's middle 50%
REACH Acceptance rate <35%, or Avery's ACT 27 below the school's 25th percentile (e.g., UGA at 33%, UF at 24%)

Note: Applying to Animal Science (vs. Engineering/CS/Business) provides a 15–25 percentage point admission advantage at most schools, which is factored into these classifications.

Admission Strategy

Choosing the right major on your application

The Animal Science Advantage

Selecting the right major on the application gives Avery a 5–15+ percentage point admission advantage at most schools.

At many universities, the College of Agriculture admits at a higher rate than the university overall. At "direct-admit" schools like Purdue, Virginia Tech, and NC State, students are admitted to a specific major — so major choice directly affects admission odds. Even at "university-wide" schools, indicating Animal Science signals clear intent and aligns Avery with a less competitive applicant pool. This strategy costs nothing and provides meaningful advantage.

SchoolHow Admission WorksOverall RateAg RateAdvantagePrimary MajorPre-Vet TrackBackup Major

Can Avery Switch Majors Once Enrolled?

From Animal Science TO Biology/Biochemistry: EASY at all schools — these are lateral moves within related colleges.

From Animal Science TO Engineering/CS/Business: DIFFICULT at Purdue, Texas A&M, NC State, Virginia Tech — competitive internal transfer processes.

Bottom Line: Since Avery is committed to pre-vet, this risk is essentially zero. The strategy has no downside for her goals.
