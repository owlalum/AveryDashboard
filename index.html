Merit Aid Notes

  • Kentucky: Automatic merit tiers based on GPA/ACT. 27 ACT + 3.85 GPA likely $16-20K/year.
  • Auburn: Competitive Presidential and Academic scholarships. $8-16K realistic.
  • Tennessee: Volunteer Scholarships automatic with stats. $8-12K likely.
  • UGA: Zell Miller (full tuition) or HOPE (partial) for Georgia residents meeting requirements.
  • Oklahoma State / Mississippi State: Generous automatic OOS merit ($9-11K/yr). Best value safeties after Kentucky.
  • FSU: Looks cheap at $37K sticker because FL has low base tuition. The $2K Vires scholarship is small, but total COA is already the lowest OOS option on the list. Larger OOS tuition waivers exist but are very competitive.
  • Purdue/Ohio State/Virginia Tech/NC State/Texas A&M: Limited OOS merit. Don't bank on it.
  • Penn State / Illinois / Wisconsin: Expensive OOS with minimal merit at these stats. $50-60K/yr realistic net cost.
  • UF: Essentially no OOS merit. Admission itself is a reach at 14% OOS acceptance.

Note: All sticker prices reflect full Cost of Attendance (tuition + room & board + fees). Merit estimates are based on the student's current GPA/ACT profile and will shift if scores change.

Ranking Formula

Each school receives a composite score from 1–5 based on four weighted factors:

Factor Weight Why This Weight
Vet School Quality & Access 35% The entire purpose of undergrad is to get into vet school
4-Year Cost 35% Must still afford 4 years of vet school after undergrad
Admission Likelihood 10% Most schools are accessible; low weight avoids over-penalizing reaches
Plan B Strength 20% If vet school doesn't happen, the degree should still have strong career value

Formula: Score = (Vet × 0.35) + (Cost × 0.35) + (Admission × 0.10) + (Plan B × 0.20)

Admission Categories

Each school is classified as SAFETY, MATCH, or REACH based on how the student's profile compares:

Category Criteria
SAFETY Acceptance rate >70%, or student's GPA and ACT are well above the school's middle 50% range
MATCH Acceptance rate 25–70%, GPA and ACT near or within the school's middle 50%
REACH Acceptance rate <25%, or student's ACT well below the school's 25th percentile (e.g., UGA ACT 32+, UF at 14% OOS)

Note: At schools with direct-admit or college-level admission (e.g., Purdue, Virginia Tech, NC State), applying to Animal Science rather than Engineering/CS/Business can provide a meaningful advantage. The degree of benefit varies by school — see the Admission Strategy tab for per-school details.

Data Sources & Assumptions

Tuition & Fees Published 2025–26 rates from each university's official website. Will need updating to 2026–27 rates when published (typically June–August 2026).
Room & Board Estimated $17,000–$21,000/year depending on location; uses school-published COA when available
Merit Aid Based on published automatic scholarship grids for the student's GPA/ACT ranges; conservative estimates used when ranges are ambiguous
Acceptance Rates Common Data Set or IPEDS most recent available year
Vet School Rankings AVMA accreditation status; U.S. News veterinary school rankings where applicable
Cost Calculation 4-Year Cost = (Annual Tuition + Room/Board + Fees − Merit Aid) × 4. Assumes no tuition increases (conservative for comparison purposes).

Admission Strategy

Choosing the right major on your application

The Animal Science Advantage

At schools with "direct-admit" systems (Purdue, Virginia Tech, NC State, etc.), students are admitted to a specific college or major — and the College of Agriculture typically admits at a higher rate than Engineering, Business, or the university overall. Even at "university-wide" schools, selecting Animal Science signals clear intent and aligns Avery with a less competitive applicant pool. This strategy costs nothing.

Data transparency: Very few schools publish college-level admission rates. Where a rate is published by the university, it is marked with a ✓ below. All other advantage assessments are qualitative, based on admission structure (direct-to-major vs. university-wide) and publicly available overall rates.

Can Avery Switch Majors Once Enrolled?

From Animal Science TO Biology/Biochemistry: EASY at all schools — these are lateral moves within related colleges.

From Animal Science TO Engineering/CS/Business: DIFFICULT at Purdue, Texas A&M, NC State, Virginia Tech — competitive internal transfer processes.

Bottom Line: Since Avery is committed to pre-vet, this risk is essentially zero. The strategy has no downside for her goals.
